Adorn Your Free-View Portrait: 3D Portrait Manipulation via Accessory Viable Field

Tzu-Chieh Liu, Wei-Chih Tu, Shao-Yi Chien
National Taiwan University
Paper Video Code

(A) To model the placement of accessories on portraits, we separately construct two distinct radiance fields: portrait geometry field and accessory viable field. By feeding different accessory geometry styles into accessory viable field, a wide variety of personalized accessories can be generated given a reference face.

(B) FOVAD can generate accessories or beards based on users' unstructured doodles, as illustrated in the yellow stroke of the thumbnail.

(C) FOVAD allows for localized texture editing and can also apply intra- or inter-domain global stylization. Moreover, as our model re-combines portraits and accessories, it can produce out-of-distribution (OOD) results, such as a man wearing earrings, a woman with a beard, or multiple accessories simultaneously.

Overview

Only a single scene-shared representation is utilized for separate modeling of portraits and accessories, based on the assumption that there is a high degree of spatial similarity between the two scenes. Therefore, a feature adaptor is used to transform scene-shared features between the portrait and accessory spaces.
